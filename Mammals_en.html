AN ORDINARY HUT

"The common cutora, or water cutora, or water shrew[1] (Latin Neomys fodiens) is a trans—Palearctic mammal species of the genus cutora, the largest shrew in Europe. Body length reaches 10 cm, tail length 8 cm; body weight — up to 20 g."

COMMON HEDGEHOG / WHITE-BREASTED HEDGEHOG

The common hedgehog is a small animal, but in general its size for the entire genus of Eurasian hedgehogs ranges from medium to large. The body length is 23.7—29 cm, the tail is about 14 to 32 mm[5][6] body weight is 700-800 g[7]. The ears are relatively small (usually from 28.5 to 40.1 mm)[5]. Common hedgehogs living in Cyprus have larger ears[8]. The muzzle is elongated. The animal's nose is sharp and constantly moist. Hedgehogs have 20 small sharp teeth on the upper jaw, and 16 on the lower jaw. The upper incisors are widely spaced, leaving room for the bite of the lower incisors. The head is relatively large, wedge-shaped, with a slightly elongated facial region

BRANDT'S NIGHT LIGHT

Brandt's moth is a small bat of the genus of moths of the family smooth—nosed, or leatherbacks (Vespertilionidae). It is named after the German zoologist Johann Brandt. It was isolated as an independent species in 1970. The body weight is 5.5–10 g, body length is 38-55 mm, tail length is 31-45 mm, forearm length is 33-39 mm, wingspan is 19-24 cm.

WATER MOTH

"The water moth has an average size for small species. The fluffy fur of the bat is brownish-gray on the back and silver-gray on the underside. Juveniles are darker in color than adults. These bats have reddish-pink faces and noses, and the eye area is bare. When a bat is agitated, its rather short ears rise at a right angle. The wings and tail membranes are dark brown. The water moth is usually 45 to 55 mm long, with an average wingspan of 240 to 275 mm. The weight ranges from 7 to 15 grams. Life expectancy can reach 22 years."
